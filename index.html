Indian snacks are a delightful explosion of flavors and textures, offering something for every palate, especially the street food favorites, which showcase the diversity and creativity of Indian cuisine. Here are my 20 personal favourite Indian snacks. Click to learn the recipe and enjoy with a cup of chai !

Recipes for Snacks

Batata vada

A popular street food from Maharashtra. The word 'Batata' is a translation of Marathi and Portuguese word for 'potato'. It is a spicy potato filling, dipped in a thick batter and fried. Best served with coriander or tamarind chutney.

Bhajia

A popular Indian snack made by deep-frying vegetables (eg; onions, potatoes, spinach and cauliflower) coated in gram flour batter. Served hot with chutneys.

Bhelpuri

A type of a chaat, known as the 'beach snack', associated with the beaches of Mumbai. The base consists of puffed rice, tossed in chopped veggies, seasoned with chat masala and salt, topped with sev. The key to its flavour is the tamarind chutney.

Bread pakora

A popular North Indian tea snack. It is made by stuffing bread slices with spiced potato filling, dipped in batter and deep-fried until crispy-golden.

Chana Chaat

Originating from Uttar Pradesh, it is made with either white or black chickpeas, added to vegetables and chutneys, seasoned with chaat masala, and topped with sev, coriander or yoghurt.

Dabeli

Originating from the Mandvi-Kutch region in Gujrat, it literally means 'pressed' in Gujrati. It is made by stuffing a spiced potato mixture into a bun and garnishing it with toppings.

Dahi vada

Also known as Dahi Bhalla, this North Indian snack is made from deep-fried lentil fritters, soaked in water, then creamy youghurt, and topped with spices and chutney. Often prepared during festivals.

Kachori

Originating from the Marwar region of Rajasthan, kachori is a deep-fried, spicy, stuffed pastry, made by stuffing a dough with spiced yellow lentils filling. Served with mint or tamarind chutney.

Kati roll

Originating from the city of Kolkata, it is a wrap filled with skewer-roasted spiced meats or vegetables, wrapped in a paratha bread, often accompanied by chutneys and onions.

Misal Pav

A 100-year old popular street food from Maharashtra, it involves 'Misal', a spicy curry made from sporuted moth beans, eaten with pav (soft bread rolls), topped with farsan.

Pani puri

Also known as Golgappa, this beloved Indian snack is made by crispy, hollow puris stuffed with flavoured stuffing, and filled with spicy, tangy and sweet water.

Pav bhaji

A popular street food from Mumbai, it originated as a quick lunctime meal for workers in Mumbai. It is a rich and flavourful vegetable curry with buttery and soft bread rolls (pav).

Puri bhaji

A classical Indian dish, involving two main components: a deep-fried puri and spiced potato curry (bhaji). Often served with a side of pickles and yoghurt.

Ragda pattice

A popular Mumbai street food that combines crispy potato patties with a flavourful white pea curry (ragda), topped with chutneys.

Rava vada

A delicious South Indian snack, made by preparing a batter of semolina, yoghurt, and spices. The batter is shaped as a doughnut and deep-fried. Served with coconut chutney.

Sabudana vada

A popular snack consumed during fasting period. It is made from soft tapioca pearls (sabudana), potatoes and peanuts, rolled into a dough. The dough is shaped into small patties and fried. Served with green or tamarind chutney.

Samosa

Originating from the Middle East, it was introduced to India by Merchants. It is a crispy, flaky pastry filled with a spicy potato or mince filling. Served hot with mint or tamarind chutney.

Vada pav

Often referred to as the 'Indian street burger' originating from Mumbai. It is a combination of fried, spicy potato dumpling (vada) sandwiched in a pav. Served hot with green chilies on the side.

Vegetable cutlet

An Indian snack made from a mix of vegetables, spices an breadcrumbs. The cutlets are shallow fried and served hot with green or tamarind chutney. It is a popular snack eaten across the globe.

Vegetable puff

A popular snack in Indian bakeries. It is made by filling a puff pastry with spiced vegetable mixture. It can either be baked or fried. Perfect for tea-time !
